Current Station

These images show the conditions of the current fire stations. These pictures show the tight fit for four engines in Station 1, built in 1972, with little room to walk or work around the equipment. Station 2, a former elementary school built in 1952, contains a single engine, offices, and a training room. The pictures of station two show the mold and water damage from the leaking roof.

Conceptual Plan

These images and renderings show a conceptual plan, developed by department members, to house all equipment and offices in one modern building. This plan is large enough to accommodate larger trucks of the future, while maintaining an efficient interior layout and exterior footprint.
